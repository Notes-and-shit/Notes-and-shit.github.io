    1. the speaker (1 mark)

    2. the context (where does the phrase occur in the plot/action?) (1 mark)

    3. the meaning (paraphrase the quote—put it into your own words) (1 mark)

    4. the significance of the quote (explain its importance through character, theme, or descriptive language (2 marks)

      Further questions to reflect and answers for question 4:

      How is the quote significant or important in terms of plot? Foreshadowing?

      What does this quote tell us about the character of the speaker?

      How does this quotation relate to a theme(s) of the play? (see Theme sheet)

      How does the descriptive language help us to understand what the character is saying?

    This analysis is mostly correct, but there is one significant issue with the interpretation of Lady Macbeth's statement. The claim that Lady Macbeth is talking down on Macbeth, saying that he lacks power, is incorrect. Instead, Lady Macbeth is questioning Macbeth's courage and masculinity, implying that he has the ambition but lacks the resolve to act upon it. This misunderstanding affects the interpretation of her character and the dynamics between her and Macbeth. So, overall, the analysis is mostly right but partially wrong due to the misinterpretation of Lady Macbeth's intention in her statement.
