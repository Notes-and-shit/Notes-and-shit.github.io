    L3 - Working With Radicals

    L3 - Working With Radicals

    Nov 22, 20243 min read

    Notes

    Important Terms

    Radical: The symbol

    Radicand: Number or expression under the radical

    Perfect Square: A Number in which the root is a whole number

    Simplifying and Rationalizing Radicals

    Simplifying

    How to simplify:

    • Rewrite as 2 factors in which one factor is a perfect square
    • Write the root of this factor in front of the other radical

    Simplified form follows these principles:

    • No factors that are perfect squares
    • Fractions in the radicand
    • Radicals in the denominator

    Rationalizing

    Method 1: One term in the denominator

    If there is simply one radical in the denominator, multiply both the numerator and denominator by that radical

    Method 2: Two terms in the denominator

    Every radical binomial has what is called a conjugate. This is a radical expression that has the same terms but an opposite operation

    Essentially you just multiply the denominator and numerator by the conjugate of the denominator

    Performing Operations With Radicals

    Multiplication

    1. Multiply all the numbers before the radical
    2. Multiply all the radicals
    3. Simplify

    Adding and Subtracting

    1. Verify that all the expressions have the same radicand - if not simplify as much as possible
    2. Add or subtract all the numbers in front of the radical and keep the radical

    Questions

    Tip

    When you move the negative from the denominator to the numerator you are multiplying the entire numerator by -1

    Tip

    To remove the denominator you can just apply it to every coefficient
