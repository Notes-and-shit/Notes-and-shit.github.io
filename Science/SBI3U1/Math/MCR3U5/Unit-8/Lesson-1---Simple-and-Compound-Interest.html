    Lesson 1 - Simple and Compound Interest

    Nov 22, 20243 min read

    1. Simple Interest

    Interest is accumulated only on the principal amount.

    or

    Where:

    • = Final amount
    • = Principal amount
    • = Interest rate (in decimal form)
    • = Time

    Example 1: Investing $250 in an account that pays 5% simple interest per year for 5 years.

    a) Determine the final amount.

    b) Determine the accumulated interest.

    2. Compound Interest

    Interest is accumulated on both the principal and the accumulated interest. Interest is added at the end of each compounding period. The variable represents the number of compounding periods per year.

    Where:

    • = Final amount
    • = Principal amount
    • = Annual interest rate (in decimal form)
    • = Time (in years)
    • = Number of compounding periods per year

    Example 2: Ali buys a new computer for $2000 (including tax). The credit card imposes 18.4% compound interest monthly. If she waits 6 months before repaying, how much does she owe and how much interest has accumulated?

    Example 3: In the next 1.5 years, you want to save $8000 for post-secondary expenses. You invest in a savings account that pays 5.25% compound interest semi-annually. How much money should you invest now and how much interest will you accumulate?

    Example 4: What quarterly compounded interest rate is needed for a $200 investment to grow to $600 in 10 years?

    Practice Problems

    Determine the final value of a $5000 investment over 3 years: a) at a simple interest rate of 4.3%.

    b) at a compound interest rate of 3.9% compounded daily.
