    Atomic Notation

    1. Define the following terms:

    • a. Atomic number: The number of protons in the nucleus of an atom, which determines the chemical element.
    • b. Mass number: The total number of protons and neutrons in the nucleus of an atom.
    • c. Isotope: Atoms of the same element with the same number of protons but different numbers of neutrons.

    2. For each of the 3 subatomic particles, answer the following:

    • Proton:
      • Location: In the nucleus.
      • Mass: Approximately 1 atomic mass unit (amu).
      • Relative mass: 1.
    • Neutron:
      • Location: In the nucleus.
      • Mass: Approximately 1 amu.
      • Relative mass: 1.
    • Electron:
      • Location: Outside the nucleus, in the electron cloud.
      • Mass: Approximately 1/1836 of a proton (very small).
      • Relative mass: ~1/1836.

    3. Identify the elements which have the following numbers of protons in the nuclei of their atoms:

    • a. 16: Sulfur (S).
    • b. 29: Copper (Cu).
    • c. 47: Silver (Ag).
    • d. 90: Thorium (Th).

    4. How many protons are in the atom of each of the following elements?

    • a. Boron (B): 5 protons.
    • b. Lead (Pb): 82 protons.
    • c. Uranium (U): 92 protons.
    • d. Calcium (Ca): 20 protons.
    • e. Silicon (Si): 14 protons.
    • f. Iron (Fe): 26 protons.
    • g. Oxygen (O): 8 protons.
    • h. Helium (He): 2 protons.
    • i. Zinc (Zn): 30 protons.
