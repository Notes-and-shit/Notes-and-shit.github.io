    In mathematics, the concepts of growth (croissance) and decay (décroissance) are often described using functions to model how a quantity increases or decreases over time.

    Growth and Decay (Croissance et Décroissance)

    Growth (Croissance)

    1. Exponential Growth: This occurs when the rate of growth is proportional to the current amount. The general form of the exponential growth function is:

    Blanktextcenter

    y(t)=y0​ekt

    where:

    • y(t) is the quantity at time t.
    • y0​ is the initial quantity.
    • k is the growth rate constant (if k>0).
    • e is the base of the natural logarithm, approximately equal to 2.71828.
    1. Linear Growth: This occurs when a quantity increases by a constant amount over equal intervals of time. The general form of the linear growth function is:

    Blanktextcenter

    y(t)=y0​+kt

    where k is a constant rate of increase.

    Decay (Décroissance)

    1. Exponential Decay: This occurs when the rate of decay is proportional to the current amount. The general form of the exponential decay function is:

    Blanktextcenter

    y(t)=y0​e−kt

    where k is the decay rate constant (if k>0).

    1. Linear Decay: This occurs when a quantity decreases by a constant amount over equal intervals of time. The general form of the linear decay function is:

    Blanktextcenter

    y(t)=y0​−kt

    where k is a constant rate of decrease.

    Applications

    • Population Growth: Often modeled by exponential growth functions when resources are unlimited.
    • Radioactive Decay: Modeled by exponential decay functions, representing how the amount of a radioactive substance decreases over time.
    • Interest Calculations: Compound interest in finance is an example of exponential growth.
    • Depreciation of Assets: The value of an asset decreasing over time can be modeled by exponential or linear decay.
