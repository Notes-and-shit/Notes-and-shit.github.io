    L1: Periodic Functions

    Notes

    What is a periodic function?

    A periodic function is a function in which the values of y repeat at regular intervals of θ.

    Example

    The height of somebody who is taking a ride on a large Ferris wheel is periodic.

    ‎

    The primary trigonometric ratios (y=sinθ, y=cosθ, y=tanθ) are periodic functions.

    Vocabulary for periodic functions

    Cycle: A complete series of y values that repeats at regular intervals (peak to peak/trough to trough)

    Period: The horizontal distance between the beginning of the cycle and the end

    Amplitude: Vertical distance between the middle of the graph and the peak/trough of the function

    Midline: Horizontal line that passes through the middle of the graph

    Phase shift: Horizontal translation of graph

    L2: Transformations of sin and cos

    Notes

    Blanktextcenter

    y=af[k(x−d)]+c

    Transformations of sin and cos

    a has an affect on:

    • amplitude
    • max/min
    • range

    k has an effect on:

    • periods
    • x-axis

    d has an effect on:

    • phase shift
    • x-axis

    c has an effect on:

    • peak/trough
    • range
    • y-axis

    Key Formulas

    amplitude =2max−min​

    period =∣k∣360°​ or ∣k∣2π​

    midline:
    y=c
    or
    c=2max+min​

    phase shift:
    (x+45°) when d=−45°
    (x−45°) when d=45°

    Transforming Axes

    Use the following steps to transform axes:

    1. Trace new axes
      y=c (Midline)
      x=d (Phase shift)
    2. Trace the curve of sin/cos
    3. Use a to determine the max/min values
      max=c+a
      min=c−a
    4. Determine the period and the distance of points
      Period=∣k∣360°​
      Distance of points=4Period​

    L3: Transformation of Tan

    Blanktextcenter

    y=af[k(x−d)]+c

    Transformations of tan

    a has an affect on:

    • 3 key points

    k has an effect on:

    • periods
    • x-axis
    • domain
    • asymptotes

    d has an effect on:

    • phase shift
    • x-axis
    • asymptotes

    c has an effect on:

    • y-axis
    • 3 key points

    Base Function

    y=tanx,−90°≤x≤90°

    • There are x-intercepts where sinx=0

    • There are vertical asymptotes where cosx=0

    tan is special, it has a default period of 180°. It also has no amplitude, we use two reference points that are at y=1 instead

    Transforming Axes

    Use the following steps to transform axes:

    1. Trace new axes
      y=c
      x=d
    2. Trace the vertical asymptotes and the 3 key points
    3. Use a to etiquette the y axis
    4. Determine the period and the distance of points
      Period=∣k∣180°​ or ∣k∣π​
      Distance of points=4Period​

    L5: Trigonometric Identities

    Key Formulas

    Reciprocal:

    cscθ=sinθ1​
    secθ=cosθ1​
    cotθ=tanθ1​

    Quotient:

    tanθ=cosθsinθ​ cotθ=cosθcosθ​

    Pythagorean:

    sin2θ+cos2θ=1

    sin2θ=1−cos2θ
    cos2θ=1−sin2θ

    1+tan2θ=sec2θ

    sec2θ−tan2θ=1
    tan2θ=sec2θ−1

    1+cot2θ=csc2θ

    1=csc2θ−cot2θ
    cot2θ=csc2θ−1
