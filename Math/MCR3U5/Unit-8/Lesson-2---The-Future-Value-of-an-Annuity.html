    Lesson 2 - The Future Value of an Annuity

    Nov 27, 20243 min read

    Notes

    What is an Ordinary Annuity?

    An Ordinary Annuity is a type of investment where equal payments are made at regular intervals over time.

    Example

    For example, at the end of each month, a regular payment is made into a savings account. The account earns interest during these months. We will focus on ordinary annuities, where::

    1. Payments are made at the end of each payment period.

    2. The compounding periods and payment periods are always the same.

    Future Value of an Ordinary Annuity

    The future value of an ordinary annuity is equal to the sum of all payments and all interest accumulated by these payments.

    Example

    For example, you invest $500 in a savings account that pays 8% interest compounded monthly. You do this each month starting next month. After 4 months, what will the account balance be?
